# Outreach targets -- 10 named beta candidates (DACH + broader EU)

**Status:** draft. Founder reviews and verifies each target before any DM lands.

**Reality check:** expect ~10% reply rate on cold DMs even when personalized. Of those who reply, maybe 30% try the product. Of those who try, maybe 30% pay. So 10 outreaches realistically yield 0--1 paying customers. The point of this list is not the conversion -- it is to get founder-credible feedback from people who actually run dev teams in the target market.

**Verification key:**
- `[VERIFIED]` -- founder has prior interaction or the entity has a stable public web presence the founder has personally seen.
- `[VERIFY-BEFORE-SEND]` -- name plausibly real but founder must confirm current handle / email / company status before contacting.
- Do not contact anyone marked `[VERIFY-BEFORE-SEND]` until verified. Use the company website, LinkedIn, or a recent talk to confirm the person is still in role and reachable.

**Channel rule:** for every target, prefer a public channel (LinkedIn comment on a relevant post, Twitter/X reply, GitHub issue) BEFORE going to DM. A cold DM after a thoughtful public reply has 3--5x the response rate.

## Workflow

1. **Verify** each `[VERIFY-BEFORE-SEND]` target -- LinkedIn current role, recent activity, correct handle. Drop and replace any that are stale.
2. **Public-touch first** for influencers (6, 7, 8, 9). Reply substantively to one of their recent posts within 7 days BEFORE the DM. The DM references the public exchange.
3. **DM cadence:** maximum 3 outreaches per day. Track in a spreadsheet (target, channel, sent date, reply, outcome).
4. **No follow-ups beyond 1.** If they do not reply within 10 days, send exactly one short bump. Then move on.
5. **Do not BCC / mass-send.** Each DM is bespoke. If you find yourself templating, you are doing it wrong.
